In 9th grade, I started a Facebook page called Micro Electronics. It was simple - I posted about electronics projects, shared PCB designs, and talked about components.

I had no idea it would grow to 20,000+ followers by the time I was 17.

Brands started reaching out. JLCPCB, NextPCB, Tuya, LCSC, and Drona Aviation all paid me for partnerships. They sent me free 3D printers, drones, and dev kits.

I turned my bedroom into a full hardware lab. Then I designed and sold STEM kits. I ran robotics workshops for kids at Gujarat Science City with WitBlox.

This was my first real taste of building something from nothing - and it taught me that you don't need a formal education to create real value. You just need to start building.
