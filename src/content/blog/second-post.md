I was born in Bareilly, a small city in Uttar Pradesh. Growing up, I loved taking things apart to understand how they worked — electronics, toys, anything I could get my hands on.

It started in 9th grade with a Facebook page called Micro Electronics. By the time I was 17, it had 20,000+ followers and brands like JLCPCB, NextPCB, Tuya, LCSC, and Drona Aviation were paying me for partnerships.

I used the money, and the free 3D printers, drones, and dev kits, to build a full hardware lab in my bedroom. Then I turned the brand into a product company, designed and sold STEM kits, and ran robotics workshops for kids.

My first real venture was NeuraliX — an attempt to make brain-computer interface hardware affordable for hobbyists. It didn't reach commercial scale, but it taught me how to ship original hardware.

Today I'm building Maya at AlterAI. The journey continues.
